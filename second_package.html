Full Itinerary

Rome to Paris European tour program redesigned for Skaria Tours and Travels.

DAY 01

Rome, Italy

Arrival in Rome

Begin the tour in Rome, the Eternal City, where ancient history, Christian heritage and Italian culture come together. After arrival, travelers settle into the journey with a comfortable transfer and an introduction to the city’s timeless atmosphere, from historic streets to impressive basilicas and monuments.

Castel Sant'Angelo

A striking riverside fortress close to Vatican City, Castel Sant’Angelo has served as a mausoleum, papal refuge and military stronghold through different periods of Roman history. Its architecture and location beside the Tiber make it one of Rome’s most atmospheric landmarks and a memorable introduction to the city.

Monument to Garibaldi

This monument honors Giuseppe Garibaldi, one of the key figures in Italian unification. Set in a scenic area with wide city views, it adds a strong historical note to the Rome sightseeing program and offers travelers a peaceful stop for photos and reflection.

Holy Stairs / Scala Santa

The Holy Stairs are among Rome’s most meaningful devotional sites, traditionally associated with the Passion of Christ. Pilgrims visit this sacred place for prayer and reflection, making it an important spiritual experience within the Rome section of the tour.

DAY 02

Rome and Vatican

Basilica of the Holy Cross

This basilica is connected with important Christian relics and has long been visited by pilgrims seeking a deeper spiritual experience in Rome. Its quiet atmosphere, religious importance and historic setting make it a meaningful stop during the pilgrimage-focused part of the journey.

St. John Lateran Basilica

St. John Lateran is one of Rome’s major papal basilicas and holds great importance in Catholic tradition. Its grand interior, sacred history and role as the cathedral church of Rome make it a powerful destination for pilgrims and visitors interested in Christian heritage.

Vatican Museums

The Vatican Museums contain one of the world’s richest collections of religious art, classical sculpture, galleries and historic treasures. Visitors experience centuries of culture and faith through beautifully decorated halls, priceless masterpieces and the artistic legacy of Vatican City.

Sistine Chapel

The Sistine Chapel is one of the most revered spaces in Vatican City, admired for its sacred atmosphere and Renaissance art. Its ceiling and altar wall are world-famous, making this a highlight for travelers who appreciate faith, history and artistic beauty.

DAY 03

Rome City Tour

Trevi Fountain

Trevi Fountain is one of Rome’s most loved landmarks, famous for its dramatic Baroque design, flowing water and lively piazza setting. It is a perfect place for photographs and for experiencing the romantic charm of the old city.

Pantheon

The Pantheon is a masterpiece of ancient Roman architecture, especially admired for its massive dome and central opening. Still standing after nearly two thousand years, it gives visitors a powerful sense of Rome’s engineering brilliance and historic continuity.

Catacombs

The Roman Catacombs offer a glimpse into early Christian history through underground burial passages and sacred spaces. This visit adds depth to the pilgrimage theme, helping travelers understand the courage, faith and traditions of the early Church communities.

St. Paul Outside the Walls

This major papal basilica is dedicated to Saint Paul and is one of Rome’s most important Christian pilgrimage churches. Its vast interior, peaceful cloisters and spiritual importance make it a deeply meaningful stop for prayer and reflection.

DAY 04

Florence and Pisa

Santa Maria del Fiore

Florence’s cathedral, Santa Maria del Fiore, is one of Italy’s greatest architectural treasures. Its grand dome, marble façade and central location reveal the artistic brilliance of the Renaissance and make it a major highlight of the Florence visit.

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo is a famous viewpoint offering sweeping views across Florence, the Arno River and the city’s historic skyline. It is an excellent photo stop and a relaxing place to admire the beauty of Tuscany’s capital.

Leaning Tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is one of Europe’s most recognizable monuments and a classic Italian highlight. Located in the Piazza dei Miracoli, it is surrounded by beautiful religious buildings and provides one of the most memorable photo opportunities of the tour.

Tuscany Drive

The drive through Tuscany adds a scenic countryside experience between Florence and Pisa. Rolling landscapes, charming towns and classic Italian views make the journey itself an enjoyable part of the day, especially for travelers who love relaxed road scenery.

DAY 05

Padua and Venice

Padua St. Anthony's Church

The Basilica of Saint Anthony in Padua is a renowned pilgrimage destination visited by devotees from around the world. Its sacred atmosphere, beautiful chapels and connection to Saint Anthony make it one of the most spiritually important stops on the itinerary.

Venice Island

Venice Island offers a magical experience of canals, bridges, narrow lanes and historic waterfront views. The floating-city atmosphere is unlike anywhere else in Europe, making Venice one of the most memorable and photogenic destinations on the route.

Piazza San Marco

Piazza San Marco is the heart of Venice, surrounded by grand architecture and famous landmarks. The square captures the elegance of the old Venetian Republic and gives travelers time to enjoy the city’s cultural charm, cafés, basilica views and lively atmosphere.

Gondola Ride Optional

The optional gondola ride offers a classic Venice experience through quiet canals and under historic bridges. It is ideal for travelers who want a romantic and traditional view of the city from the water.

DAY 06

Postojna, Slovenia

Postojna Cave

Postojna Cave is one of Slovenia’s most famous natural attractions, known for its underground passages, dramatic rock formations and cave-train experience. It gives travelers a completely different landscape experience after the city-focused Italian portion of the tour.

Predjama Castle

Predjama Castle is a remarkable fortress built into a cave cliff, creating one of Europe’s most unusual castle settings. Its dramatic location and medieval character make it a strong visual highlight of the Slovenia visit.

DAY 07

Salzburg, Austria

Salzburg City

Salzburg is a beautiful Austrian city known for music, baroque architecture, old streets and alpine surroundings. The city offers a graceful mix of culture and scenery, making it a pleasant stop between Slovenia and Germany.

Alpine Route

The alpine route through Austria gives travelers scenic mountain views, green valleys and peaceful countryside landscapes. This journey adds a relaxing nature element to the tour and offers many memorable views from the road.

DAY 08

Munich, Germany

Munich City Tour

Munich introduces travelers to Bavaria’s cultural capital, with old squares, elegant buildings, churches and a lively urban atmosphere. The city tour gives a quick but enjoyable look at Germany’s heritage, architecture and clean city planning.

German Heritage

This portion of the journey highlights Germany’s organized cities, cultural traditions and historic character. It serves as a gentle transition from the alpine landscapes toward the Swiss and Black Forest sections of the tour.

DAY 09

Lucerne, Switzerland

Lucerne

Lucerne is one of Switzerland’s most charming cities, known for its lake, mountain backdrop, wooden bridge, old town and peaceful Swiss atmosphere. It is a major scenic highlight and a beautiful place for relaxed sightseeing and photography.

Swiss Lake Views

The lake views around Lucerne showcase Switzerland’s calm natural beauty, with clear waters framed by alpine peaks. This stop allows travelers to enjoy the slower, peaceful side of Switzerland after several busy sightseeing days.

DAY 10

Titisee, Germany

Titisee

Titisee is a scenic lake in Germany’s Black Forest region, popular for its calm water, lakeside walks, shops and cafés. The destination combines natural beauty with the relaxed charm of a traditional German holiday town.

Black Forest

The Black Forest is known for dense woodland, traditional villages, cuckoo clocks and peaceful countryside scenery. It gives travelers a refreshing nature break and a taste of one of Germany’s most beloved regions.

DAY 11

Interlaken, Switzerland

Interlaken

Interlaken is a famous Swiss resort town located between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, surrounded by dramatic alpine peaks. It is a major highlight for mountain views, Swiss scenery and optional adventure experiences.

Swiss Alps Experience

This experience focuses on the beauty of Switzerland’s alpine landscapes, including mountain backdrops, clean streets and peaceful surroundings. It is perfect for photography and for enjoying the calm charm of Swiss travel.

DAY 12

Paris, France

Arrival in Paris

Arrive in Paris, the City of Lights, known for elegance, culture, fashion, monuments and romantic city views. This day gives travelers a relaxed introduction before the main sightseeing program begins.

Evening Paris

Evening in Paris brings a special charm as monuments, bridges and avenues begin to glow. It is a lovely time to enjoy the atmosphere of the city, take photos and experience Paris at a slower pace.

DAY 13

Paris City Tour

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is the most famous symbol of Paris and one of the world’s most recognized landmarks. Whether admired from the ground or visited for panoramic views, it is the unforgettable highlight of the Paris city tour.

Paris Landmarks

Paris sightseeing includes elegant boulevards, historic bridges, monuments and beautiful city views. This stop gives travelers a broader impression of the French capital beyond a single attraction.

Church Visit

A church visit in Paris provides time for prayer, quiet reflection and appreciation of sacred architecture. It continues the pilgrimage character of the tour while allowing travelers to experience the spiritual side of the city.

Paris Shopping Time

Shopping time in Paris gives travelers a chance to explore local stores, souvenir shops and fashionable streets at a relaxed pace. It is also a good opportunity for photos, cafés and free time before departure.

DAY 14

Departure

Final Memories

The final day is a time to look back on the journey from Rome to Paris, covering sacred churches, historic cities, alpine landscapes and world-famous landmarks. Travelers depart with lasting memories of Europe’s faith, culture and scenery.

Airport Transfer

After completing the 13-night, 14-day Europe tour, proceed to the airport for the return journey. The transfer is arranged for a comfortable conclusion to the program, allowing travelers to end the tour smoothly and peacefully.
