Day Wise Itinerary

Important highlights include image cards, while supporting stops are presented as detailed text cards for a cleaner and more premium itinerary.

01
DAY 01

Napoli, Italy to Bari

Napoli Arrival

Arrive at Napoli airport, meet the tour representative and begin the scenic drive towards Bari. The day is planned as a smooth arrival and transfer day, giving guests time to settle in before the overnight ferry journey.

Bari to Albania Ferry

Board the overnight cruise ferry from Bari to Durrës. This Adriatic Sea crossing gives the group a relaxed travel experience, with time to rest before beginning the Balkan sightseeing route in Albania.

02
DAY 02

Durrës to Struga, Macedonia

Skanderbeg Square, Tirana

Visit Tirana’s central landmark, named after Albania’s national hero Gjergj Kastrioti Skënderbeu. The square is surrounded by important civic buildings, museums and open public spaces that introduce the capital’s history and modern culture.

Struga Overnight

Continue towards Struga, a calm lakeside town near Lake Ohrid. This stop is ideal for an overnight break after the Albania route and prepares the group for the next day’s journey to Skopje.

03
DAY 03

Struga to Skopje

Skopje City

Explore Skopje, the capital of North Macedonia, where Roman, Byzantine and Ottoman influences meet modern city squares and monuments. The city offers a mix of historic lanes, riverside views and lively public spaces.

Memorial House of Mother Teresa

Visit the memorial dedicated to Mother Teresa, who was born in Skopje. The site presents her life, humanitarian work and spiritual legacy through exhibits, photographs and a peaceful chapel atmosphere.

The Stone Bridge

See the Stone Bridge across the Vardar River, one of Skopje’s most recognized landmarks. It links the old and new parts of the city and offers a memorable photo stop during the city orientation.

04
DAY 04

Skopje, Macedonia

Mustafa Pasha Mosque

An Ottoman-era mosque located near Skopje’s Old Bazaar. Its elegant architecture and elevated setting make it a meaningful optional stop for guests interested in the city’s Islamic heritage.

The Millennium Cross

Visit or view the 66-metre Millennium Cross on Vodno Mountain, built to mark 2,000 years of Christianity. The area is known for panoramic views over Skopje and the surrounding mountain landscape.

Matka Canyon

Enjoy Matka Canyon, one of the most scenic natural attractions near Skopje. The area is known for dramatic cliffs, lake views, monasteries and optional boat rides through the canyon.

05
DAY 05

Skopje to Sofia, Bulgaria

Sofia City

Arrive in Sofia, Bulgaria’s capital, a city shaped by Greek, Roman, Ottoman and Soviet-era influences. The orientation introduces broad boulevards, historic churches and government buildings.

Alexander Nevsky Cathedral

Visit the grand gold-domed Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, one of Sofia’s most famous landmarks. Its impressive exterior, icon-filled interior and Orthodox heritage make it a key highlight of Bulgaria.

Arena Armeec

Pass by Arena Armeec, Sofia’s major indoor sports and event venue. It is included as a city reference point during the wider drive through the Bulgarian capital.

06
DAY 06

Sofia to Bucharest, Romania

Bucharest

Arrive in Bucharest, Romania’s capital and commercial centre. The city combines elegant boulevards, historic architecture, communist-era landmarks and a lively urban atmosphere.

Parliament Palace

See the Palace of Parliament, one of Bucharest’s most striking landmarks. Built during Romania’s communist period, the massive structure reflects the scale and ambition of that era.

Revolution Square

Visit Revolution Square, an important historic area connected to the events of 1989 and the fall of Nicolae Ceaușescu. It is a meaningful stop for understanding modern Romanian history.

Primaverii Palace

Explore or view the former private residence of Nicolae and Elena Ceaușescu. The palace gives visitors a closer look at the lifestyle and political history of Romania’s communist leadership.

07
DAY 07

Bucharest Sightseeing

Metropolitan Church

Visit the Metropolitan Church area on Mitropoliei Hill, a key centre of Romanian Orthodox faith. The peaceful surroundings and religious architecture add cultural depth to the Bucharest sightseeing day.

Carol I Park

Enjoy Carol I Park, a landscaped city park with monuments, walking paths and the eternal flame at the grave of the Unknown Soldier. It offers a quieter break within the capital.

08
DAY 08

Bucharest to Transylvania

Peleș Castle

Visit Peleș Castle in Sinaia, a Neo-Renaissance royal residence built for King Carol I. Its mountain setting, ornate rooms and fairytale architecture make it one of Romania’s most beautiful attractions.

Bran Castle / Dracula Castle

Discover Bran Castle, widely associated with the Dracula legend. Beyond the myth, the castle offers medieval architecture, hilltop views and a strong connection to Transylvanian tourism.

Council Square, Brașov

Walk through Council Square in Brașov, surrounded by colourful buildings, old streets and medieval charm. It is a pleasant stop for photos, cafés and a relaxed city atmosphere.

09
DAY 09

Brașov to Cluj-Napoca

Rupea Fortress

See Rupea Fortress, a medieval stronghold built by Transylvanian Saxons on a high basalt hill. The site adds a dramatic historical stop between Brașov and Cluj-Napoca.

Sighișoara

Visit Sighișoara, one of Transylvania’s best-preserved historic towns and a UNESCO-listed destination. Its cobbled streets, towers and colourful houses create a classic medieval atmosphere.

10
DAY 10

Cluj-Napoca to Satu Mare

Romania Scenic Drive

Travel from Cluj-Napoca to Satu Mare through Romania’s countryside, passing regional towns and rural landscapes. This day is mainly a comfortable transfer with scenic views along the route.

Satu Mare Overnight

Stay overnight in Satu Mare and relax before crossing towards Hungary. The stop keeps the route comfortable and balances the longer travel sections of the programme.

11
DAY 11

Satu Mare to Budapest, Hungary

Buda Castle & Castle Hill

Explore Buda Castle and Castle Hill, the historic palace district overlooking the Danube. The area features museums, monuments, old streets and excellent viewpoints over Budapest.

Parliament Buildings

Admire the Hungarian Parliament Building, Budapest’s magnificent Neo-Gothic landmark beside the Danube. It is one of the city’s most photographed sights and a highlight of the riverfront skyline.

Fisherman's Bastion

Visit Fisherman’s Bastion, a Neo-Romanesque terrace with towers and courtyards overlooking the Danube. It offers some of the finest views of Pest and the Parliament Building.

Stalin Monument Site

See the historic site connected with Hungary’s 1956 Revolution and anti-Soviet protests. This stop gives context to Budapest’s modern political history and national memory.

12
DAY 12

Budapest Sightseeing

Heroes' Square

Visit Heroes’ Square, home to the Millennium Monument and statues of important Hungarian leaders. It is one of Budapest’s grand ceremonial spaces and a key city landmark.

Danube River Cruise

Enjoy a scenic Danube River cruise and view Budapest from the water. The route offers memorable perspectives of Buda Castle, Parliament, bridges and the illuminated riverfront.

Danube Promenade

Walk along the Danube Promenade between Elisabeth Bridge and Széchenyi Chain Bridge. It is a relaxed riverside experience with classic views of Buda and Pest.

Gellért Hill

Visit or view Gellért Hill, a panoramic hill above the Danube known for city views, monuments and nearby thermal-spring heritage. It is a rewarding photo stop in Budapest.

13
DAY 13

Slovenia

Ljubljana

Discover Ljubljana, Slovenia’s charming capital, known for its riverside old town, bridges, cafés and relaxed walking streets. The city offers a softer, elegant contrast after the larger capitals.

Ljubljana Castle

Visit Ljubljana Castle on Castle Hill above the old town. The castle area gives excellent views across the city and introduces Slovenia’s medieval and cultural history.

Postojna Cave

Experience Postojna Cave, one of Slovenia’s most famous natural attractions. Its underground train ride, vast chambers and dramatic rock formations make it a major highlight of the tour.

Predjama Castle

See Predjama Castle, a spectacular fortress built into a cliff near Postojna. Its unusual setting and legendary stories make it one of Slovenia’s most memorable photo stops.

14
DAY 14

Venice, Italy Departure

Venice Airport Transfer

Check out and proceed towards Venice airport for the departure flight. The final transfer completes the route from Southern Italy through the Balkans, Central Europe and Slovenia back to Italy.

Tour Complete

Conclude the Eastern Europe Grand Tour with memories of historic cities, castles, churches, caves, river views and scenic drives across seven countries.
